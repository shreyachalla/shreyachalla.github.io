About

I enjoy tennis, reading (some of my favorites here!), and going on runs.

Learning & Reading

Selected Undergraduate Coursework

  • Digital System Design: Switch and gate design, boolean algebra, arithmetic, number systems, digital design principles.
  • Programming Hardware/Software Systems: Creation of complex execution and storage mechanisms, based on instruction set architecture, for software design including high-level programming languages and operating systems.
  • Data Structures and Algorithms for Applications: Computer data structures and algorithms in the context of object-oriented programming.
  • Digital Design Laboratory: Designing & implementing digital systems utilizing CAD tools, logic synthesis, and assembly language programming.
  • Physical Foundations of Computer Engineering: Basic principles governing the physical realization of computing systems and their relationship to characteristics such as performance, energy, and robustness. Implementation technologies.
  • Architecture, Systems, Concurrency, and Energy in Computation: Major concepts of processors, including memory hierarchy and operating system constructs; lab activities done in Verilog and SystemVerilog.
  • Objects and Design: Object-oriented programming methods for dealing with large programs. Focus on development processes including debugging and testing techniques.
  • Design and Analysis of Algorithms: Understanding efficient algorithms for standard computational problems, including Graph Theory, Dynamic Programming, and NP-Completeness.
  • Intro to Electronic Systems Packaging: Introduction to packaging technologies, technology drivers, electrical performance, thermal management, materials, optoelectronics, RF integration, reliability, system issues, assembly, testing.
  • Design of Operating Systems: Multi-threading, scheduling, file systems. Implementing features to the Linux-like xv6 teaching operating system.
  • Cloud Computing: Cloud architectures, system design, design methodologies, Amazon Web Services.
  • Embedded Systems: I/O devices, device drivers, RTOS, using Mbed microcontroller and Raspberry Pi.
  • VLSI & Digital Design: Transistor behavior, power and performance analysis, adder/multipler topologies. Modeling with Cadence Virtuoso.
  • Processor Design: Principles in pipelined processor design, with focus on architecture.
  • Compilers: Study of techniques for the design and implementation of front-end, back-end, and middle-end of compilers.

Selected Graduate Coursework

  • Advanced Computer Architecture: Comprehensive study of high performance computer architecture emphasizing quantitative assessment of design decisions.
  • Fundamentals of Machine Learning: Study of classification, regression, convolutional neural networks, clustering, and autoencoders, with a focus on PyTorch and applications.
  • Computer Communication Networks: Computer network architecture & protocols.
  • Advanced Programming Techniques: Programming using OpenMP and MPI (multithreading), OPenGL (graphics), and SFML (multimedia).
  • DSP for Speech Synthesis: DSP concepts for speech communication applications, including homomorphic processing, linear predictive coding, time & frequency domain analysis.
  • Micrelectromechanical Devices: Mechanical and thermal behaviors, fabrication methods, and transduction techniques for MEMS.
  • Wireless Power Transmission & Sensing: Survey of basic radiation, propagation, RF energy harvesting, inductive systems, basic communications theory.
  • HW/SW Co-Design for ML Systems: Survey of HW accelerators for DNNs, compute/memory constraints, distributed training.
